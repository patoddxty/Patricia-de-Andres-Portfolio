Have you seen this girl? Illustration
This illustration reflects on the relationship between social media and the loss of privacy in the construction of identity. The protagonist appears taking a selfie, her face partially hidden by a red rectangle symbolising the mobile phone screen, acting as a filter and barrier. This visual gesture suggests how part of one's personality is diluted or hidden in digital environments. The text ‘Have you seen this girl?’ refers both to advertising language—which objectifies the image—and to wanted posters, casting an almost persecutory gaze on the figure portrayed. The illustration was created in Adobe Photoshop, using a Wacom tablet for manual drawing. The final composition and its adaptation to editorial format were developed in Adobe InDesign using a magazine mock-up.
